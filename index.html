About

I'm a 22 year old graduate of the University of Virginia. Whereas my background is in politics, my work experience, however, is in tech. Born and raised in Brooklyn, NY, my long-term goal is to be a Architectural Technologist using my technical experience in engineering to develop algorithms, software, products, and modernizing current products to make them "smart."

Work

FORGE 2019 - 2020

I first joined HackCville (before the name change to Forge) in 2019 for a skill course named "Hustle" designed to give participants the business and entrepreneurial fundamentals to operate a business. After finishing a Intro to CS course in Python, I decided to move onto Node: a Data Science track aimed at designing machine learning models, cleaning, analyzing, and visualizing data; columinating in presentations and reports delivering our conclusions. After completeing the skills course, I worked for C4K and University Virginia Sustainability in 2/3 sprints (the pandemic interrupted all activities after the second sprint). For C4K, I prototyped a KKN (K-Nearest Neighbors) model to predict future numbers and identified the ages, trends, and reasons for students departing the C4K program. For University Virginia Sustainability I collaborated alongside four analysts to designate 5 areas where sustainability can improve to meet their 2025 waste reduction targets.

BRICKHOUSE SOLUTIONS & NEST SEEKERS SUMMER 2019

For the summer of 2019, I interned under Elizabeth Perea under her company Brickhouse Solutions as a web developer. I stripped her entire website back and started over from scratch picking out colors, designing the layout, tranferring the domain, and text. I tranferred content designing front pages and website navigating tools using SEO, CCS, HTML; performed unit testing at page level. As Ms. Perea is a real estate, agent, coach, podcaster, and interviewer and we often moved from office to office as she held coaching sessions at Nest Seekers and met with clients. On those occasions where she held coaching sessions, Ms. Perea allowed me to advise clients on how to optimize their leads by curating content for their social media accounts. I advised that Pinterest should be used to showcase their taste in interior design, twitter should be used to converse, connect, and share information, in addition to posting beautiful photos of beautiful properties. While amassing a follwing on Twitter, real agents become THE recognized realtor in their respective locations. Instagram is person but should also provide an experience that is reflective of the clients they wish to attract. If the theme is too polarizing with the identity of the realtor, they should create a seperate instagram account.
